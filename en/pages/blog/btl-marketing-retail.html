The Power of BTL Marketing in Retail

What Is BTL Marketing and Why It Works

BTL (Below The Line) marketing encompasses direct, targeted marketing activities that establish immediate contact with consumers. Unlike ATL (Above The Line) advertising, which includes television and radio, BTL focuses on specific activities at the point of sale — promotional campaigns, tastings, merchandising, and events. The advantage of BTL marketing is that it reaches the consumer at the moment of purchase decision — when they are already in the store and ready to buy. Studies show that 70% of purchases are impulsive decisions, which further increases the effectiveness of BTL activities.

Promotional Campaigns and Tastings

Product tasting is one of the most effective BTL tools, especially in the food and beverage industry. When a consumer tries a product before buying, the likelihood of purchase increases dramatically — according to some studies, up to 65%. During tastings, the promoter's professionalism is crucial — they should know the product's features, be able to answer consumer questions, and naturally guide the conversation. The visual appeal of the promotional stand is also critical — it should attract attention and reflect the brand identity.

Merchandising: Winning on the Shelf

Merchandising is the art of product placement in the retail environment, directly impacting sales. Proper product positioning on the shelf — at eye level, to the right of the entrance, near the checkout — can increase sales by 30-50%. POS (Point of Sale) materials — wobblers, shelf talkers, floor stands — attract consumer attention and accelerate purchase decisions. Seasonal displays, cross-merchandising (for example, chips next to beer), and last-minute impulse purchase zones near the checkout — all of this is part of a BTL strategy that significantly boosts sales.

Measuring BTL Campaign Results

One of the main advantages of BTL marketing is its measurability. The results of every activity can be measured in specific numbers: how many people participated in the tasting, how many purchased the product, what was the ROI (return on investment). Analyzing sales data before, during, and after a promotional campaign reveals the campaign's real impact. It's also important to measure the long-term effect — did the product maintain increased sales after the promotion ended? The best BTL campaign is one that not only drives one-time sales but also acquires new loyal customers.
